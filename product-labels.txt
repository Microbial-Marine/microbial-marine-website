---- EASY TRACE ---- 
Concept: 
MICROBIAL MARINE EASY TRACE is a solution that contains 15 major and trace elements. These elements have been selected based on their relevance to coral health in scientific literature, as well as the success of large coral aquaculture facilities that have reported using them. EASY TRACE uses only USP- and ACS-grade ingredients, ensuring the highest quality possible. EASY TRACE provides a scientifically backed and convenient way to add rapidly consumed major and trace elements without the risk of overdose and without raising alkalinity, calcium, or magnesium. Perfect for nano aquariums or systems designed for ease of use. Shelf stable for over a year. Dosing pump compatible.


Instructions:
Apply 1 mL per 10 gallons (40 liters) daily for regular use. This is a safe dose with no risk of overdose. The dose can be increased beyond 1 mL per 10 gallons (40 liters), with adjustments based on ICP test results.


Ingredients (mg/g of product):
Bromide (0.24), Iodine (0.22), Potassium (0.18), Boron (0.16), Iron (0.08), Manganese (0.07), Sulfur (0.002), Molybdenum (0.002), Vanadium (0.0006) Nickel (0.0005), Rubidium (0.0003), Zinc (0.0001), Selenium (0.00005), Chromium (0.00005), Cobalt (0.00003), Chelating Compounds, Stabilizers, Water


Does not contain EDTA or other strong
chelators.




-- NITROUS --
Concept:
MICROBIAL MARINE NITROUS is a full-spectrum nitrogen supplement. Corals have been shown to selectively uptake ammonium and urea as their primary nitrogen sources, rather than nitrate. Many studies have also demonstrated that nitrate addition can cause stress to corals
by altering the ideal population of zooxanthellae in their tissue. NITROUS uses USP- and
ACS-grade ingredients to deliver ammonium and urea to your reef. Faster growth, better coloration, and denser tissue are widely reported among those who have switched to ammonium + urea dosing. NITROUS can also be used to start new aquariums, cycling them faster compared to using ammonium alone, due to its wider nutrient profile. Shelf stable for over a year. Dosing pump compatible.




Instructions:
Apply 1 mL per 10 gallons (40 liters) daily for regular use. This is a safe dose and will deliver ~ 0.1 mg/L of ammonia (0.36 ppm of nitrate upon conversion.) Apply 2 mL per gallon (4 liters) ONCE if cycling an aquarium. If transitioning from dosing nitrate, slowly increase the dose of NITROUS weekly by 1–2 mL and monitor nitrate levels until the desired level is reached. When first using NITROUS instead of nitrate, you may observe a decrease in nitrate until the dose of NITROUS is scaled up.




Ingredients (mg/g of product):
Ammonium (9.86), Urea (2,46), Water, Stabilizers.




--- REDUCE DOC ---
Concept:
MICROBIAL MARINE REDUCE DOC/ORGANICS is a blend of specialty coconut-based, low-dust, steam-activated carbon and macroporous adsorption resin. The inclusion of macroporous adsorption resin gives this product superior performance in removing refractory (long-chain) organic compounds. Long-chain organics can yellow the water, reduce PAR, bind to useful trace elements (rendering them unavailable to the animals in your reef), and even create chemical conditions that favor the growth of dinoflagellates. In addition to its refractory removal capabilities, this product can also remove labile (short-chain) organics from the system. These organics are easily degraded compounds that act as food for bacteria in the tank, which can lower ORP and oxygen levels and shift your system to a “microbialized” state - an undesirable condition for coral growth. Beyond long- and short-chain organics, this product is also excellent for removing odor-causing compounds, medications, and other organic contaminants such as pesticides, herbicides, and likely even toxins produced by corals, cyanobacteria, dinoflagellates, and flatworms. It can also be used on the intake of a skimmer or in an air filter to prevent airborne volatile organic compounds (VOCs) from entering your system. This blend is also ideal for use when running ozone, as it removes dangerous oxidized byproducts and excess ozone.


Instructions:
Use the included bag and run on a 1-2 week on/off cycle. If tests indicate high levels of DOC or organic contamination, a reactor can be used to lower these levels more aggressively. If the system has a high continual organic input or test values show high DOC levels, the media can be run passively. If you choose to run the product passively, use a smaller amount than usual to reduce the risk of stripping too many organics from the system. Running passively can create a "ceiling" for organics, helping to prevent overdosing of common additives such as carbon dosing, amino acids, vitamins, or coral and fish foods.


Dosage:
* Low DOC = 25 mL or less per 10 gallons
* Moderate DOC = 50 mL or less per 10 gallons
* High DOC = 100 mL or less per 10 gallons 


Technical (carbon):
* Surface Area (BET) = 1050 m2/g
* Ash Content = 4% (max)
* Iodine Number = 1000 mg/g (min)
* CTC Adsorption = 50% (min)
* Moisture Content = 5% (max)
* Ball-pan Hardness = 98% (min)
* Density = 430 - 540 kg/m3


Technical (macroporous adsorption resin):
* TOC Capacity = 5 - 20 grams per liter of resin (min)
* Total Capacity = 0.8 eq/L 7.5 Kgr/ft3 (min)
* Particle Size Range = 300 - 1200 µm
* Uniformity Coefficient = 1.7 (max)
